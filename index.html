The history of the blue link

The internet is now part of every aspect of our lives, but have you ever noticed that links are blue? Let’s discover together the origin of this convention.

1964

Project Xanadu

Ted Nelson’s Xanadu project introduced the concept of hypertext with links that visually connect two pages.

1983

HyperTIES

The HyperTIES system used cyan text on a black background. Perhaps an ancestor of the blue link.

1985

Windows 1.0

Windows 1.0 introduced the first underlining for clickable elements and used dark blue for borders and titles.

1987

WWW

The first version of the World Wide Web featured underlined links, but without any specific color.

1993

Mosaic

The Mosaic browser finally introduced the blue link! A convention that persists to this day.
